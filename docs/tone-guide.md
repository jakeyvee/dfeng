# Admin tone-of-voice guide — Dongfeng Experience Community

**Linear ticket:** VOL-200

Short enough to glance at while you moderate. Pin it in your admin chat. The goal:
sound like a helpful owner in the group, not a brand account.

---

## The voice in one line

**Friendly, welcoming, knowledgeable, owner-led.** We're enthusiasts helping
enthusiasts — not a sales desk, not a call centre, not a robot.

## Five quick rules

1. **Talk like a person.** Warm, plain English. Singaporean-friendly is great;
   light "lah/leh" is fine if it's natural — never forced.
2. **Owner-led, not brand-led.** Share and invite ("a lot of owners find…",
   "happy to help"), don't pitch ("our award-winning vehicle…").
3. **Light emoji.** A little goes a long way. 🧡 is our community accent —
   use it as a signature touch, not in every line.
4. **Helpful before anything else.** Answer the question first; route to the
   right topic second. No upselling.
5. **Be brief and kind.** Even when correcting someone, stay friendly. Assume
   good intent.

## Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Good question — a few owners here have hit this, let me grab the details." | "Thank you for reaching out to us. Your enquiry is important." |
| "Welcome! 🧡 Glad to have you." | "Welcome to the official Dongfeng community channel." |
| "That one's better in Support & Assistance — they'll sort you out fast." | "Please be advised this is the incorrect channel for your query." |
| "Honestly, range is solid for daily drives — owners can share real numbers." | "Our class-leading range delivers an unrivalled ownership experience." |
| Use 🧡 occasionally as a signature | Stack emojis (🎉🔥🚗💯) or use them in every message |
| Admit when you'll check and come back | Over-promise or give corporate non-answers |

## Sample rewrites (corporate → community voice)

- **Corporate:** "We appreciate your interest in our vehicles. A representative
  will assist you shortly."
  **Community:** "Glad you're keen 🧡 — ask away and the owners here (or one of
  us) will jump in."

- **Corporate:** "This matter falls outside the scope of this channel. Kindly
  redirect your enquiry accordingly."
  **Community:** "This one's a Support & Assistance thing — pop it in there and
  they'll get you sorted."

- **Corporate:** "Our cutting-edge EV technology offers best-in-class
  performance and value."
  **Community:** "It's a genuinely good drive — but don't take our word for it,
  the owners here will give you the straight story."

- **Corporate:** "Your feedback has been duly noted and escalated to the
  relevant department."
  **Community:** "Thanks for flagging — I'll check with the team and come back
  to you here."

## When you're moderating

- Nudge, don't scold. "Let's keep this one in the BOX Lounge 🙂" beats a warning.
- Redirect with a reason: tell them *which* topic and *why* it'll get them helped
  faster.
- Repeated spam/abuse → follow the moderation runbook; keep public messages calm
  and short.

## Brand palette note

Primary palette: **Dark Blue, Red, White.** Community accent: **orange / 🧡**.

- Use the **🧡 orange accent** as the community's identifier — in welcome lines,
  pinned messages, and as an occasional signature. It's what makes this feel like
  *our* space rather than a generic corporate channel.
- Keep Dark Blue / Red / White as the primary brand frame for any designed assets
  (avatar, banners, event graphics).
- ⚠️ **Launch risk (design-lock):** the PRD flagged the Q8 brand-colour phrasing
  as ambiguous — the exact relationship between the primary palette and the
  orange community accent isn't locked. Treat orange as the **community accent
  only** until stakeholders confirm. Full risk recorded in
  `docs/branding-assets.md`.
